About Me


I am a full-stack developer coding bootcamp student with the University of Minnesota looking for full time work in information technology or web development. I reside in Minneapolis and my ultimate goal is to work in Norway. I have been studying Norwegian since 2018 and I also have professional experience using Spanish.

I studied sociocultural anthropology at Hamline University, with research focused on the anthropology of sound and noise pollution. I had the opportunity to take a 2017 short course in Siem Reap, Cambodia to learn about the blossoming tourist industry around the Angkor temple complex.

I enjoy photography for fun and the sites I visited in Cambodia provided me with some beautiful photos. A couple other the locations I've enjoyed shooting include the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness in northeastern Minnesota, and the Apostle Islands off of Wisconsin's Lake Superior shoreline.

Another area of interest for me is audio engineering, espeically in the context of experimental music. In my undergraduate education I took audio engineering knowledge from music to make sensory ethnography work, or documentaries of human culture focused on the senses. My final project was to record the soundscape of Hidden Falls Regional Park in St. Paul, MN, juxtaposing urban noise with nature sound to learn about how people conceive of nature through sound.

Thank you for visiting — my web development portfolio, sound work, and photography can all be found on this site.
